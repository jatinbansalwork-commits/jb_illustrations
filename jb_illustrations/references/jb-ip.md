# JB IP

## Character Definition

JB is the fixed visual IP for English inline illustrations in this skill.

JB appears in every image by default. JB is not a mascot, not a sticker, not cute decoration — JB is an absurd worker seriously participating in how the system runs.

Same character can play different **roles** in a scene (see `character-roles.md`).

## Appearance — light mode

- Small solid-dark creature (deep indigo-black, hand-drawn).
- Two **pointy ears** with a small tuft between them.
- White dot eyes — no mouth, no nose, no emoji face.
- Thin legs and occasional thin arms.
- Body can be cylindrical, bean-shaped, or slightly irregular hand-drawn outline.
- Expression: blank, deadpan, calm, serious.

## Appearance — dark mode

- **White hand-drawn outline** on near-black background (`#0a0a0a`–`#1a1a1a`).
- Same silhouette: pointy ears, tuft, two white dot eyes, thin white stick limbs.
- Not a solid filled blob on dark — use white outline treatment.
- Posture carries emotion, not facial detail.

Light and dark are the same JB — only background polarity changes.

## Personality

- Very serious about absurd work.
- Like a quiet system operator at a chai tapri or a back-office desk.
- Dry humor — never cute.
- A bit clumsy, never stupid.
- Looks like they actually own one job on a whiteboard sketch.

## Role casting

When a scene needs actors, cast JB in distinct roles — same base silhouette, different props/posture. Prefer Bangalore everyday settings:

| Role | Visual cue | Typical action |
|------|------------|----------------|
| **Worker / Vendor** | steel tumbler, yellow auto, decoction flask | overwhelmed, running routes, auto queue |
| **Owner / Approver** | stamp, weighing scale, counter desk | signs off route, approves order, final check |
| **Runner** | cart rope, sorting rack lever | sorts boxes, feeds the hub, keeps flow moving |
| **Fixer** | clipboard, jugaad wire, warning sign | manual fixes, label chaos, queue problems |
| **Customer** | speech bubble, off to the side | states the order or intent |

Use **1–3 JB instances max** per image. One distinguishing prop each.

## Common actions

- Carrying raw material.
- Pulling wires to merge information sources.
- Stuck at a breakpoint.
- Operating a judgment lever inside a machine.
- Becoming a sorting funnel.
- Stamping handoff copy or approval block.
- Holding an onboarding path like a rope.
- Holding a warning sign at a pit.
- Bricklaying, bridging, opening doors, sorting, logging.
- Buried in a pile of mismatched locality boards and route cards.

## Indian Cultural Flavor (When It Fits)

JB can interact with low-tech Indian everyday objects when the metaphor calls for it:

- Steel coffee tumbler + davara, filter coffee flask, autorickshaw meter, BMTC route board.
- Jugaad wire fix, KR Market weighing scale, post-office stamp pad.
- Auto stand locality board, tapri counter, bus-stop bench.

Use 1–2 objects max. Don't turn every image into a cultural postcard — the metaphor must serve the article.

## Taboos

- Don't draw JB as an overly cute mascot.
- Don't make a children's cartoon character.
- No elaborate costumes — one simple prop max per role.
- No emoji faces or sparkly eyes.
- Don't leave JB watching from a corner.
- Don't let JB overshadow the structure being explained.
- Don't change the base design per role (no different species, no human realism).
- Don't rely on stereotypes (sacred imagery, caricatured accents, clichéd kitsch).

## Quality Bar

If removing JB leaves the core metaphor fully intact, JB is too decorative — rewrite the prompt so JB is the action subject.
